# Subject Pronouns

| Pronoun | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| `Nüü` | I |
| `Taa` | You & I |
| `Üü` | You |
| `Mahu` | He/She (nearby, visible) |
| `Uhu` | He/She (far, not present) |

---

# Some Simple Verbs

| Verb | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| `Tüka` | to eat |
| `Hibi` | to drink |
| `Katü` | to sit |
| `Mia` | to go |

Word order is flexible — the subject can come before or after the verb. The `-ti` suffix marks present tense.

`Nüü tüka-ti` = `Tüka-ti nüü` = *I am eating*

---

Hibi-ti üü. | You are drinking.
Üü mia-ti. | You are going.
Mahu tüka-ti. | He/She is eating.
Katü-ti taa. | You and I are sitting.
Tüka-ti üü. | You are eating.
Hibi-ti mahu. | He/She is drinking.
Katü-ti nüü. | I am sitting.
Uhu mia-ti. | He is going.

---

Nüü hibi-ti. | I am drinking.
Tüka-ti taa. | You and I are eating.
Mia-ti mahu. | He is going.
Mia-ti uhu. | She is going.
Katü-ti nüü. | I am sitting.
Mahu hibi-ti. | He is drinking.
Üü mia-ti. | You are going.
Mahu tüka-ti. | She is eating.

---

# Subject Pronouns — Plurals

| Pronoun | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| `Nüügwa` | I & others |
| `Taagwa` | You & I & others |
| `Üügwa` | You & others |
| `Mahuŵa` | He/She (nearby) & others |
| `Uhuŵa` | He/She (far) & others |

---

Hibi-ti üügwa. | You all (you & others) are drinking.
Üügwa mia-ti. | You all are going.
Mahuŵa tüka-ti. | They (he & others) are eating.
Katü-ti uhuŵa. | They (she & others) are sitting.
Tüka-ti üügwa. | You all are eating.
Hibi-ti mahuŵa. | They (she & others) are drinking.
Katü-ti nüügwa. | We (I & others) are sitting.
Taagwa mia-ti. | We (you & I & others) are going.

---

# More Verbs — Superbowl Edition!

| Verb | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| `Wichimui` | to play football |
| `Poyoha` | to run |
| `Tagwüna'i` | to throw |
| `Poono'i` | to play ball (any ball sport) |

---

Wichimui-ti üügwa. | You all (you & others) are playing football.
Poyoha-ti üü. | You are running.
Mahuŵa poono'i-ti. | They (he & others) are playing ball.
Tagwüna'i-ti uhuŵa. | They (she & others) are throwing.
Nüü poyoha-ti. | I am running.
Wichimui-ti uhuŵa. | They (she & others) are playing football.
Taagwa poono'i-ti. | We (you & I & others) are playing ball.
Nüügwa poyoha-ti. | We (I & others) are running.
