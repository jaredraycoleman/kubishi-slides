# Subject Noun Suffixes

`-uu` — far away (not visible)
`-ii` — nearby (visible)

**Examples**
- `Isha'-uu` — the coyote / that coyote (not visible)
- `Isha'-ii` — the coyote / this coyote (visible)

---

Hibi-ti isha'-uu. | The coyote is drinking.
Padühidda-ii mia-ti. | The elk is going.
Pahabichi-uu tüka-ti. | The bear is eating.
Kamü-ii katü-ti. | The jackrabbit is sitting.
Tüka-ti huuna'-ii. | The badger is eating.
Hibi-ti tühidda-ii. | The deer is drinking.
Katü-ti pungizhi-uu. | The mouse is sitting.
Kooipa-uu mia-ti. | The Bighorn sheep is going.

---

Wichimui-ti isha'-uu. | The coyote is playing football.
Padühidda-ii poyoha-ti. | The elk is running.
Pahabichi-uu poono'i-ti. | The bear is playing ball.
Kamü-ii tagwüna'i-ti. | The jackrabbit is throwing.
Poono'i-ti huuna'-ii. | The badger is playing ball.
Wichimui-ti tühidda-ii. | The deer is playing football.
Tagwüna'i-ti pungizhi-uu. | The mouse is throwing.
Kooipa-uu poyoha-ti. | The Bighorn sheep is running.
