# Tens — `X-wano`

Build the tens by adding `-wano` to a number root (Lesson 9):

| # | Root | Ten |
|---|------|-----|
| 20 | `waha` (2) | `wahawano` |
| 30 | `pahi` (3) | `pahiwano` |
| 40 | `watsügwi` (4) | `watsügwiwano` |
| 50 | `manügi` (5) | `manügiwano` |
| 60 | `naabahi` (6) | `naabahiwano` |
| 70 | `tatsüwi` (7) | `tatsüwiwano` |
| 80 | `woosüwi` (8) | `woosüwiwano` |
| 90 | `kwaanuki` (9) | `kwaanukiwano` |

---

Wahawano. | twenty (20)
Pahiwano. | thirty (30)
Watsügwiwano. | forty (40)
Manügiwano. | fifty (50)
Naabahiwano. | sixty (60)
Tatsüwiwano. | seventy (70)
Woosüwiwano. | eighty (80)
Kwaanukiwano. | ninety (90)

# In-between numbers

For a number like 23, name the **ten**, then the **ones** with `-ma`, then `tsibui-gü-dü` (literally *"it climbs up to / it comes to"*):

`Wahawano pahi-ma tsibui-gü-dü.` — *It is 23.*

Pattern: **[ten] + [ones]`-ma` + `tsibui-gü-dü`**

---

Wahawano pahi-ma tsibui-gü-dü. | It is 23.
Pahiwano waha-ma tsibui-gü-dü. | It is 32.
Watsügwiwano manügi-ma tsibui-gü-dü. | It is 45.
Manügiwano sümü'-ma tsibui-gü-dü. | It is 51.
Naabahiwano naabahi-ma tsibui-gü-dü. | It is 66.
Tatsüwiwano woosüwi-ma tsibui-gü-dü. | It is 78.
Kwaanukiwano kwaanuki-ma tsibui-gü-dü. | It is 99.
