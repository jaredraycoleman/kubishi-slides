# The `-du` / `-gu` rule

Every number except `sümü'` (one) takes an ending that depends on **what the number is describing**:

| Ending | Use it when the number describes the… | Example |
|--------|----------------------------------------|---------|
| `-du` | **subject** (the do-er) | `watsügwi-du` — four |
| `-gu` | **object** (the thing acted on) | `pahi-gu` — three |

Same number, two jobs:

`Nüügwa watsügwi-du.` — *We are four.*
`Nüü pahi-gu tühidda-neika a-buni-ti.` — *I see three deer.*

---

# Describing the subject (`-du`)

When the number tells you about the **do-er**, add `-du`. Use it with the subject pronouns from Lesson 1.

---

Nüügwa watsügwi-du. | We are four.
Üügwa waha-du. | You (all) are two.
Taagwa pahi-du. | We (you & I & others) are three.
Uhuŵa manügi-du. | They are five.
Üügwa naabahi-du. | You (all) are six.

---

# Counting what you see (`-gu`)

When the number tells you about the **thing acted on** — the object — add `-gu`. Put the number right before the object noun, then the verb (Lesson 5 pattern).

`Nüü pahi-gu tühidda-neika a-buni-ti.` — *I see three deer.*

---

Nüü pahi-gu tühidda-neika a-buni-ti. | I see three deer.
Nüü waha-gu isha'-neika a-buni-ti. | I see two coyotes.
Nüü watsügwi-gu kamü-neika a-buni-ti. | I see four jackrabbits.
Nüü manügi-gu tsiipa'-neika a-buni-ti. | I see five birds.
Nüü naabahi-gu püyü-neika a-buni-ti. | I see six ducks.
Nüü waha-gu kotsokotso'-neika a-buni-ti. | I see two blue jays.
