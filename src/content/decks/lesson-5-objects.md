# Object Suffixes

`-oka/-noka` — far away
`-eika/-neika` — nearby

**Examples**
- `Isha'-oka` — the coyote / that coyote (not visible)
- `Isha'-eika` — the coyote / this coyote (visible)

**Vocab**
- Tüka — to eat
- Hibi — to drink
- Puni — to see
- Yadohi — to talk
- Paya — water
- Tüba — pine nuts

---

Isha'-uu hibi-ti. | The coyote is drinking.
Isha'-uu paya-nieka a-hibi-ti. | The coyote is drinking the (nearby) water.
Paya-nieka isha'-uu a-hibi-ti. | The (nearby) water — the coyote is drinking it.
Pahabichi-ii puni-ti. | The bear sees.
Pahabichi-ii tüba-neika a-düka-ti. | The bear is eating the (nearby) pine nuts.
Kamü-ii i-buni-ti. | The jackrabbit sees me.
Huuna'-ii pungizhi-noka u-yadohi-ti. | The badger talks to that (far) mouse.
Pungizhi-ii huuna'-oka u-yadohi-ti. | The mouse talks to that (far) badger.
Mahu paya-neika a-hibi-ti. | He/she is drinking the (nearby) water.
Kamü-uu kadu'u tüba-neika a-düka-ti. | The (far) jackrabbit isn't eating the (nearby) pine nuts.
