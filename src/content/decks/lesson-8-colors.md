# Colors — `X-banagi-`

`X-banagi-` is a verb meaning *to be the color X*. The color **is** the predicate — there's no separate word for "to be." Attach a color root to `-banagi-`:

| Root | Color | Verb |
|------|-------|------|
| `aka` | red | `aka-banagi-` |
| `oha` | yellow | `oha-banagi-` |
| `otü` | brown | `otü-banagi-` |
| `puhi` | blue / green | `puhi-banagi-` |
| `tuhu` | black | `tuhu-banagi-` |
| `tosa` | white | `tosa-banagi-` |

---

# Blue or green? `puhi` vs `puhidua`

`puhi-banagi-` covers **both blue and green**. When you specifically mean the *green of a growing plant*, the root `puhidua-` is used instead:

- `Puhibü-uu puhidua-dü.` — *The green shrub is green.*

So: sky and bluebird → `puhi`; a green leaf → `puhidua`.

---

# Saying what color something is

Add `-dü` ("the one that is…") to the color verb. This is the present-tense, stative form — like the `-dü` you saw in Lesson 7.

`Mahu puhi-banagi-dü.` — *He / she / it is blue.*

Swap in any color, or any subject (a pronoun, or an animal you already know).

---

# Colors on animals you know

The color verb works just like any other verb — subject (with `-uu` / `-ii`) plus the `-banagi-dü` form.

# "I see the ___ one"

Colors combine with what you know from Lesson 4 (objects) and Lesson 5. Add `-ika` to the color form to make it the object, then `a-buni-ti` — *I see it*.

`Nüü aka-banagi-dü-ika a-buni-ti.` — *I see the red one.*

This is the pattern for "find the ___ one" games — point at a card and ask.

---

# A fun one: rainbows

`padoogogwünü-` — *to cast a rainbow.*

`Padoogogwünü-ti.` — *It's casting a rainbow. / There's a rainbow out.*
