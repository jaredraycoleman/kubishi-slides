# Verb Tense Suffixes

| Suffix | Tense |
|--------|-------|
| `-ti` | present |
| `-ku` | completive (past) |
| `-wei` | future |

The suffix attaches to the verb. The rest of the sentence — subjects, objects, possessives — stays the same.

---

# One verb, three tenses

`Hibi-ti isha'-uu` — *the coyote is drinking*
`Hibi-ku isha'-uu` — *the coyote drank*
`Hibi-wei isha'-uu` — *the coyote will drink*

---

# Past tense (-ku)

Take the present-tense sentences you already know and swap `-ti` for `-ku`.

---

Hibi-ku isha'-uu. | The coyote drank.
Padühidda-ii mia-ku. | The elk went.
Pahabichi-uu tüka-ku. | The bear ate.
Kamü-ii katü-ku. | The jackrabbit sat.
Tüka-ku huuna'-ii. | The badger ate.
Hibi-ku tühidda-ii. | The deer drank.
Katü-ku pungizhi-uu. | The mouse sat.
Kooipa-uu mia-ku. | The Bighorn sheep went.

---

Wichimui-ku isha'-uu. | The coyote played football.
Padühidda-ii poyoha-ku. | The elk ran.
Pahabichi-uu poono'i-ku. | The bear played ball.
Kamü-ii tagwüna'i-ku. | The jackrabbit threw.
Kooipa-uu poyoha-ku. | The Bighorn sheep ran.

---

# Future tense (-wei)

Same idea — swap `-ti` for `-wei`.

---

Hibi-wei isha'-uu. | The coyote will drink.
Padühidda-ii mia-wei. | The elk will go.
Pahabichi-uu tüka-wei. | The bear will eat.
Kamü-ii katü-wei. | The jackrabbit will sit.
Tüka-wei huuna'-ii. | The badger will eat.
Hibi-wei tühidda-ii. | The deer will drink.
Katü-wei pungizhi-uu. | The mouse will sit.
Kooipa-uu mia-wei. | The Bighorn sheep will go.

---

Wichimui-wei isha'-uu. | The coyote will play football.
Padühidda-ii poyoha-wei. | The elk will run.
Pahabichi-uu poono'i-wei. | The bear will play ball.
Kamü-ii tagwüna'i-wei. | The jackrabbit will throw.
Kooipa-uu poyoha-wei. | The Bighorn sheep will run.

---

# With objects (Lesson 5)

Object phrases stay the same — only the verb suffix changes.

---

Isha'-uu paya-nieka a-hibi-ku. | The coyote drank the water.
Isha'-uu paya-nieka a-hibi-wei. | The coyote will drink the water.
Pahabichi-ii tüba-neika a-düka-ku. | The bear ate the pine nuts.
Pahabichi-ii tüba-neika a-düka-wei. | The bear will eat the pine nuts.
Kamü-ii i-buni-ku. | The jackrabbit saw me.
Kamü-ii i-buni-wei. | The jackrabbit will see me.
Huuna'-ii pungizhi-noka u-yadohi-ku. | The badger talked to that mouse.
Huuna'-ii pungizhi-noka u-yadohi-wei. | The badger will talk to that mouse.

---

# With possessives (Lesson 6)

---

I-isha'bugu-uu tüka-ku. | My dog ate.
I-isha'bugu-uu tüka-wei. | My dog will eat.
Pahabichi-uu i-düba-neika a-düka-ku. | The bear ate my pine nuts.
Pahabichi-uu i-düba-neika a-düka-wei. | The bear will eat my pine nuts.
Ü-bagwi-uu i-bagwi-noka u-düka-ku. | Your fish ate my fish.
Ü-bagwi-uu i-bagwi-noka u-düka-wei. | Your fish will eat my fish.
Ma-dabuutsi'-uu poyoha-ku. | His cottontail ran.
Ma-dabuutsi'-uu poyoha-wei. | His cottontail will run.
Nüü u-kaw̃a-noka u-buni-ku. | I saw his wood rat.
Nüü u-kaw̃a-noka u-buni-wei. | I will see his wood rat.
